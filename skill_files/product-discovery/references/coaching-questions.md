# PM Coaching Questions by Section

This is a comprehensive question bank organized by discovery section. Use these questions to help teams think deeper and ensure thorough discovery.

## Business Problem

### Understanding the Problem Core

- What specific problem are you trying to solve?
- How would you describe this problem to someone unfamiliar with your business?
- What triggered this discovery? Why now?
- Is this a new problem or a long-standing one we're finally addressing?

### Identifying Who Is Affected

- Who specifically experiences this problem? (Can you name roles or teams?)
- How many people are affected? (Rough estimate)
- Are some people affected more than others?
- Who would be most frustrated by this problem today?

### Understanding Frequency & Scope

- How often does this problem occur?
- When does it happen? (At specific times, under certain conditions?)
- What triggers the problem?
- Is it happening to everyone every time, or only in specific scenarios?

### Getting Concrete Examples

- Walk me through a specific time this problem occurred
- Can you show me an example from the past week?
- What was the person trying to do?
- What went wrong?
- What did they have to do as a workaround?

### Measuring Business Impact

- What's the business impact if we don't solve this?
- How much time does this waste? (hours/person/period?)
- Does it cause errors? Quality issues? Revenue impact?
- What's the cost of these workarounds or errors?
- Can you quantify the impact in dollars?

### Validating the Problem

- How do you know this is a problem? (observation? data? feedback?)
- Who else has mentioned this? How many people?
- What's your evidence? (research, tickets, complaints, metrics?)
- Have you observed users struggling with this, or are they telling you about it?
- Do you have data showing this is happening at scale?

### Exploring Existing Approaches

- What have people tried as workarounds?
- Is the current system able to do what people need, or does it just require more work?
- Why haven't people solved this already?
- What would prevent them from changing their approach?

### Red Flags (Push Back on These)

When you hear these, dig deeper:
- "Users are frustrated" → Ask: Which users? How do you know?
- "We get a lot of complaints" → Ask: What's the actual volume? From whom?
- "It's an obvious problem" → Ask: How do you know it's obvious? Who agrees?
- "Everyone wants this feature" → Ask: Who's everyone? Have you asked them?
- "It's slowing us down" → Ask: By how much? In which scenarios?

## Expected Outcomes

### Defining Success

- What will be different if this problem is solved?
- What should the user/team be able to do?
- How will you know the problem is solved?
- What's success in measurable terms?

### Connecting to Business Value

- What business problem does solving this address?
- How does this support our strategy/OKRs?
- What's the business case? (revenue? cost? risk? efficiency?)
- Why should the company invest in this?

### Quantifying Impact

- Can you put a number on the benefit?
- What would you measure to show value?
- What's the current baseline? (how it is now)
- What's the target? (how it should be)

### Validation

- Who in the business cares about this outcome?
- Have you validated that these are the right outcomes with stakeholders?
- Would the business approve investment based on these outcomes?
- Are these outcomes realistic and achievable?

### Timeline

- When should these outcomes be achieved?
- Is that timing realistic?
- How will launch timeline affect when outcomes appear?

## Stakeholders & Teams

### Identifying Key Stakeholders

- Who uses/experiences the problem? (users/operators/teams?)
- Who makes decisions about this? (approver/sponsor?)
- Who will implement this? (engineering, design, operations?)
- Who will be impacted by changes? (adjacent teams, support, ops?)
- Who in the business cares about success? (executive sponsor?)

### Understanding Roles

- What role does each person play?
- Who needs to approve this work?
- Who will do the implementation?
- Who will maintain this after launch?
- Whose buy-in is critical for success?

### Red Flags

- Only one team represented → Encourage including impacted teams
- No business stakeholder → Who will approve the business case?
- No user voice → Need to include actual users/operators
- No exec sponsor → Who will unblock resources?

## Use Cases & Requirements

### Understanding Core Scenarios

- What's the main thing users need to do?
- Walk me through that scenario step-by-step
- What are the 2-3 most important use cases?
- Are there variations or edge cases?

### Distinguishing Business vs. Functional Requirements

Remember: In discovery, focus on business requirements (what problem), not functional requirements (how to build).

- What does the solution need to do? (business requirement)
- How would we build it? (functional requirement - too early!)
- What outcome does the user need? (business requirement)
- What UI/interaction pattern should it use? (design decision - too early!)

### Prioritizing

- Which use cases are must-have for launch?
- Which are nice-to-have?
- What could we launch without and add later?
- What's the minimum to solve the core problem?

### Validation

- Have you validated these use cases with users?
- Do users agree these are important?
- Are there use cases you're missing?

## Discovery Execution

### Research Planning

- How will you understand the problem deeply?
- Who do you need to talk to?
- What research methods make sense? (interviews? data analysis? observation?)
- How many people do you need to talk to?

### Timeline

- When will discovery happen?
- How much time do you need?
- When does the decision need to be made?
- Is this timeline realistic?

### Feasibility Validation

- Do you need to validate any assumptions upfront?
- Are there technical feasibility concerns to explore?
- Do you need a technical spike or proof-of-concept?

### Stakeholder Alignment

- Are stakeholders aligned on the problem?
- Do they agree on the timeline?
- Who needs to approve moving to solution design?

---

## Current Process Understanding

### Mapping Current State

- How does the current process work today?
- Walk me through it step-by-step
- What systems/tools are involved?
- Where does it break down?
- What are the pain points?

### Understanding Why Things Work the Way They Do

- Why is the current process designed this way?
- Are there constraints or dependencies?
- What's preventing them from doing it differently?

---

## Measurement & Success

### Defining Metrics

- How will you measure if this worked?
- What's the key metric?
- Are there secondary metrics?

### Baseline & Target

- What's the current state? (baseline)
- What should it be? (target)
- Is the target realistic?

### Measurement Approach

- How will you collect the data?
- Who will track it?
- How often will you measure?
- When will you assess success?

### ROI/Business Case

- How will you calculate ROI?
- What's the expected value?
- What's the cost?
- Does the benefit justify the cost?

---

## Risk & Assumptions

### Identifying Assumptions

- What are you assuming is true?
- Have you validated these assumptions?
- What would happen if they're wrong?

### Identifying Risks

- What could go wrong?
- How likely is it?
- What's the impact if it happens?
- How will you mitigate it?

### Open Questions

- What don't you know yet?
- How will you find out?
- When do you need to know?

---

## Overall Discovery Health Check

These questions help assess if a discovery is ready for viability call:

- Is the problem clearly defined and specific?
- Do you have evidence backing up the problem?
- Are expected outcomes measurable?
- Are stakeholders aligned on the problem and approach?
- Do you understand the current state and future state?
- Are use cases prioritized and realistic?
- Can you measure success?
- Have you identified and planned for major risks?
- Is the team ready to move to solution design?
- Would a smart executive approve this based on the evidence?

## Coaching Technique: The Socratic Method

Use these patterns to help teams discover insights themselves:

**When they say something vague:**
- Them: "Users are frustrated"
- You: "Which users specifically? Can you give me an example?"

**When they're making an assumption:**
- Them: "Everyone wants this feature"
- You: "Have you talked to users about this? What did they say?"

**When they're jumping to solutions:**
- Them: "We need to build a dashboard"
- You: "What's the business problem we're trying to solve with a dashboard?"

**When they're missing scope:**
- Them: "Let's improve reporting"
- You: "Who specifically needs the improvement? What does good look like?"

**When they lack evidence:**
- Them: "This is slowing us down"
- You: "How much slower? Do you have data? How do you measure it?"

**When they haven't validated:**
- Them: "Users will love this"
- You: "Have you talked to users about this? What did they say?"

## Questions by Role

### For Product Managers

- Does this solve a real user/business problem?
- Is this aligned to strategy/OKRs?
- Do you have evidence of the problem?
- Can you articulate why this matters now?
- What's the business value?
- How will you measure success?

### For Designers

- Who is the user for this capability?
- What are their current workflows?
- Where do they struggle?
- What would better look like?
- Have you talked to users?

### For Engineering Leaders

- Is this technically feasible?
- Are there integration/dependencies?
- What's the technical risk?
- Do we have capacity?
- What's the estimated effort?

### For Business Stakeholders

- What's the business case?
- What's the ROI?
- How does this support strategy?
- What's the timeline?
- What's the investment required?

## Anti-Patterns to Challenge

These are red flags that indicate deeper discovery is needed:

| Anti-Pattern | What to Ask |
|---|---|
| "Build X because Y said so" | Have you validated this with users? What research backs this up? |
| "We need this ASAP" | Why? What problem are we solving? How urgent is it really? |
| "Everyone wants this" | How many is everyone? Have you talked to them? What specifically do they want? |
| "Make it faster/better/easier" | Faster/better/easier than what? By how much? What would good look like? |
| "Copy what competitor X does" | Why does X do this? Does it solve a problem for them? Would it solve ours? |
| "User feedback says..." | How many users? Did you validate? Or is this one person's opinion? |
| "Let's build and learn" | What are you trying to learn? Could we learn cheaper through discovery first? |
| "It's obvious" | How do you know? Have you asked users? Have you looked at data? |

## Deeper Dives

### For Complex/High-Risk Discoveries

Ask additional questions:

- What's the worst case if we get this wrong?
- What assumptions are critical to validate?
- What could delay or block this?
- Do we have executive alignment?
- What are we betting on?
- How will we know early if we're wrong?

### For Customer-Facing Products

Ask additional questions:

- Have you talked to customers?
- What do they say the problem is?
- Would they pay for a solution?
- Are there adoption risks?
- What's the competitive context?

### For Internal Tools/Processes

Ask additional questions:

- What's the current process?
- Why is it inefficient?
- Who would use the new capability?
- What's the change management challenge?
- Will teams adopt this?

---

## Summary

Strong discovery conversations:
- ✅ Ask open-ended questions first, narrow down to specifics
- ✅ Challenge vague statements with examples and specifics
- ✅ Look for evidence, not opinions
- ✅ Connect problems to business value
- ✅ Make assumptions explicit and plan to validate them
- ✅ Understand current state before jumping to solutions
- ✅ Define measurable success upfront
