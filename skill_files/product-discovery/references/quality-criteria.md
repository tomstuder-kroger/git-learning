# Discovery Quality Assessment Criteria

Use this rubric to assess discovery quality and provide feedback to teams.

---

## Overall Quality Levels

### Level 1: Insufficient ❌

**Status**: Do not proceed to viability call. Return to discovery.

**Characteristics**:
- Missing critical sections or information
- Problem statement is vague or unclear
- No evidence or validation
- Outcomes not measurable
- Team not aligned

**What to do:**
- Return to discovery phase
- Focus on strengthening weak areas before re-assessment
- Ask probing questions to help team think deeper

**Example**: "The problem statement needs specificity. Right now it's 'improve reporting' - but for whom, in what way, and how much should it improve?"

### Level 2: Needs Work ⚠️

**Status**: Discovery is in progress. Make progress on gaps before viability call.

**Characteristics**:
- Core sections present but lacking detail
- Problem is identified but not fully specific
- Some evidence but could be stronger
- Outcomes defined but not all measurable
- Stakeholder alignment partial

**What to do:**
- Identify the 2-3 most important gaps
- Provide specific guidance on how to improve
- Suggest follow-up research or stakeholder conversations
- Offer to help refine specific sections

**Example**: "Your problem statement is good - you've identified that margin reporting takes 8 hours/month. The next step is to dig deeper into why: Is it system limitation? Process inefficiency? Lack of visibility? That clarity will shape your solution design."

### Level 3: Good ✓

**Status**: Ready for viability call. Sufficient detail to make informed decision.

**Characteristics**:
- All essential sections complete
- Problem is specific and backed by some evidence
- Outcomes are measurable
- Team is aligned
- Risks identified

**What to do:**
- Ready for viability call with business stakeholders
- Can proceed to solution design
- May have minor refinements but not blockers

**Example**: "This discovery is solid. You've clearly defined the problem, you have evidence from user interviews and data, your outcomes are measurable, and stakeholders are aligned. Ready for viability call."

### Level 4: Excellent 🌟

**Status**: Exemplary discovery. Ready for viability call and future reference.

**Characteristics**:
- Comprehensive and thorough
- Strong evidence backing every claim
- Measurable outcomes with realistic targets
- Clear stakeholder alignment
- Risks identified and mitigated
- Could serve as example for other teams

**What to do:**
- Ready for viability call
- Ready for solution design
- Consider as case study or template for other discoveries
- Share learnings with broader team

**Example**: "Excellent discovery. You have clear evidence from 12 user interviews and 3 months of transaction data, specific outcomes with realistic targets, stakeholder alignment from CFO to warehouse operators, and a thoughtful risk mitigation plan. This is ready for viability call and solution design."

## Section-by-Section Rubric

Use this rubric to assess individual sections and provide specific feedback.

### Discovery Summary

#### Insufficient ❌
- Missing team members or roles unclear
- No OKR connection
- Status not tracked

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Complete team but roles unclear
- Vague OKR alignment ("strategic importance")
- Status present but not updated

#### Good ✓
- Clear team roster with defined roles
- Specific OKR alignment
- Current status tracked
- Timeline clear

#### Excellent 🌟
- Clear team + stakeholders identified
- Strong OKR alignment with specifics
- Status tracked and current
- Realistic timeline with milestones

---

### Business Problem

#### Insufficient ❌
- Generic problem statement ("improve X")
- No specific users or scenarios
- No evidence provided
- Vague about impact

**Feedback**: "Your problem statement is too generic. Instead of 'improve efficiency,' can you tell me: Who specifically? Doing what? How much time does it take? What's the impact?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Problem identified but lacks specificity
- Vague about users or scenarios
- Minimal evidence (one person's feedback)
- Impact estimated but not quantified

**Feedback**: "Good start - you've identified that reporting takes time. The next step: How much time exactly? Which people? What specifically slows them down? Do you have data or have you talked to users?"

#### Good ✓
- Specific problem with clear user impact
- 2-3 concrete examples provided
- Some evidence (user feedback or data)
- Quantified impact (time, cost, error rate)
- Clear who is affected and how often

**Feedback**: "Clear problem statement backed by evidence. You've identified that F&A spends 8 hours/month on margin reconciliation, you have user examples, and you understand the business impact ($2M in potential mis-pricing). Good foundation for the rest of discovery."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Precise, specific problem statement
- Multiple concrete examples
- Strong evidence (research, data, multiple sources)
- Quantified impact with business context
- Clear about who, when, how often, and why

**Feedback**: "Excellent problem articulation. You have evidence from 10 user interviews, 3 months of reconciliation logs, and financial audit findings. Your examples are specific and relatable. The business impact is quantified. This is the foundation solution design can build on."

---

### Expected Outcomes

#### Insufficient ❌
- Vague outcomes ("improve X", "better Y")
- No metrics or how to measure
- No baseline or targets

**Feedback**: "Your outcomes are too vague. Instead of 'improve margin reporting,' let's define: What specifically will improve? How will you measure it? What's the current baseline? What's the target?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Outcomes identified but not fully measurable
- Metrics mentioned but no baseline/target
- Timeline unclear

**Feedback**: "You've identified the right outcomes, but they need to be more measurable. 'Reduce time' should be 'Reduce from 8 hours to <1 hour.' What's your baseline? What's your realistic target?"

#### Good ✓
- Specific, measurable outcomes
- Success metrics defined with baselines and targets
- Realistic and time-bound
- Connected to business value

**Feedback**: "Clear outcomes with measurable targets. Reducing reconciliation from 8 to <1 hour is specific. Your targets are realistic given the scope. Good alignment to business goals."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Clear, specific, measurable outcomes
- Multiple success metrics with realistic targets and timelines
- Strong business value articulation
- Outcomes are ambitious but achievable

**Feedback**: "Excellent outcome definition. You have specific metrics (reconciliation time, accuracy %, reporting date), realistic targets backed by expected solution capability, and clear timeline. This gives solution design a clear north star."

---

### Stakeholders & Teams

#### Insufficient ❌
- Only PM perspective
- No user voice
- No business stakeholder
- Unclear roles

**Feedback**: "You need more stakeholder voices. Who are the actual users? Who makes the business decision? Who will implement? Include them in discovery."

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Most stakeholders identified but incomplete
- Roles somewhat unclear
- Missing one critical perspective (user OR business OR technical)

**Feedback**: "Good stakeholder list. You're missing the business stakeholder who will approve the investment. Who's the CFO or budget owner who needs to buy in?"

#### Good ✓
- Comprehensive stakeholder list (users, business, technical, impacted teams)
- Roles clearly defined
- Key decision-makers identified

**Feedback**: "Good stakeholder map. You have user voice (category managers), business stakeholder (CFO), technical perspective (system architect), and implementation team (engineering). All perspectives covered."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Comprehensive stakeholder map
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Key decision-makers and influencers identified
- Early engagement shown

**Feedback**: "Excellent stakeholder engagement. You've included operators, decision-makers, technical experts, and impacted teams. Roles are clear. You're already collaborating on discovery. This ensures alignment through solution design."

---

### Use Cases & Business Requirements

#### Insufficient ❌
- No specific use cases
- Vague requirements
- No prioritization
- Confusing features with requirements

**Feedback**: "Let's define specific use cases. Instead of 'view margin data,' describe: Who? Doing what? What's the outcome they need? What makes it successful?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Use cases identified but not fully detailed
- Requirements present but not clearly prioritized
- Some confusion between business and functional requirements

**Feedback**: "Good use cases, but they need more detail. For 'view margin data' - what information? In what timeframe? What does the user do with the info? How often?"

#### Good ✓
- 2-3 primary use cases with specific scenarios
- Business requirements (not technical) clearly stated
- Prioritized or weighted
- Traceable to business problem

**Feedback**: "Clear primary use cases grounded in the business problem. You've prioritized them. They're stated as business needs (what problem solved) not features. Ready for design."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Multiple use cases with rich detail
- Clear scenarios with user goals
- Well-prioritized (must-have, should-have, nice-to-have)
- Some edge cases identified
- Business requirements clearly separated from design decisions

**Feedback**: "Excellent use case definition. You have 3 primary use cases with detailed scenarios, well-prioritized, and separated from design. You've even identified edge cases (late discount scenarios). Design has clear scope."

---

### Current Process Understanding

#### Insufficient ❌
- No documented current process
- Unclear where problems occur
- No data about current state

**Feedback**: "Help me understand the current state. Walk me through how margin reporting works today step-by-step. Where does it break?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Current process documented but lacks detail
- Pain points identified but not fully understood
- Limited data about current state

**Feedback**: "Good overview of current process. Can you add more detail on the systems involved? How long does each step take? Where specifically does the 8-hour reconciliation happen?"

#### Good ✓
- Clear documentation of current process
- Key pain points identified
- Understands why current state exists
- Some baseline data

**Feedback**: "Clear current state understanding. You've documented the 3-step process, identified the reconciliation bottleneck, and understand the system limitations. Good foundation for future state design."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Detailed current process map
- Pain points clearly identified with data
- Understanding of constraints and dependencies
- Baseline metrics for comparison

**Feedback**: "Excellent current state analysis. Your process flow is detailed, you have data on time/errors at each step, you understand system constraints, and you have baseline metrics. Design can clearly see the opportunity."

---

### Envisioned Future State

#### Insufficient ❌
- No future state described
- Only happy path, no edge cases
- Unclear how improvement happens

**Feedback**: "What will the future look like? Walk me through the improved scenario. How does it solve the original problem?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Future state described but lacking detail
- Happy path only
- Unclear how it integrates with existing systems

**Feedback**: "Good future state vision. Can you add more detail? What systems are involved? What happens if there's an error? How does it compare to current?"

#### Good ✓
- Clear future state scenario described
- Shows improvement over current
- Includes main interactions/systems
- Some edge case consideration

**Feedback**: "Clear future state. Margin report loads in <5 seconds, calculation is automatic, drill-down works easily. Big improvement over current. Good vision for design."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Detailed future state with artifacts (flows, diagrams)
- Shows full journey (success + error paths)
- Clear system integration
- Before/after comparison
- End-to-end context

**Feedback**: "Excellent future state vision. Your process flow shows end-to-end journey, you have before/after comparison, system integration is clear, and you've considered edge cases. Design has clear target."

---

### Measurement Plan

#### Insufficient ❌
- No metrics defined
- No baseline or targets
- No plan for measurement

**Feedback**: "How will you know if this worked? What will you measure? What's success?"

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Metrics mentioned but not complete
- No baseline or targets specified
- Measurement approach unclear

**Feedback**: "Good metrics identified. But what's the baseline? What's the target? How will you collect the data? Who will track it?"

#### Good ✓
- Success metrics defined
- Baseline and target specified
- Measurement approach feasible
- Timeline for measurement

**Feedback**: "Clear measurement plan. You'll track reconciliation time (baseline 8 hrs, target <1 hr) and accuracy (87% → 95%). Time tracking and variance testing are feasible approaches. Ready to measure post-launch."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Multiple success metrics with realistic targets
- Clear baseline and target values
- Feasible, specific measurement approach
- Assigned ownership
- Timeline and decision criteria defined

**Feedback**: "Excellent measurement plan. You have multiple metrics, specific baselines/targets, clear how you'll collect data (time tracking, variance testing), assigned ownership, and defined success criteria. Post-launch learning is built in."

---

### Open Questions & Risks

#### Insufficient ❌
- No risks identified
- No open questions
- Assumes everything will go smoothly

**Feedback**: "What could go wrong? What don't you know? Let's identify risks and open questions upfront."

#### Needs Work ⚠️
- Some risks identified but no mitigation plan
- Open questions documented but no plan to address
- May be missing major risks

**Feedback**: "Good risk identification. Now for each risk, what's the mitigation? If this assumption is wrong, how will you find out? What's your contingency?"

#### Good ✓
- Key risks identified with impact/probability
- Mitigation plans for major risks
- Open questions documented
- Plan to address unknowns

**Feedback**: "Good risk awareness. You've identified technical feasibility as critical, planned a spike to validate, and identified adoption as a risk with mitigation plan. Prepared for obstacles."

#### Excellent 🌟
- Comprehensive risk identification with impact/probability
- Clear mitigation strategy for each
- Open questions with investigation plan
- Assumptions explicitly called out
- Monitoring plan post-launch

**Feedback**: "Excellent risk management. You've comprehensively identified risks (technical, organizational, data), prioritized by impact, planned specific mitigation (technical spike, change management), and have monitoring plan. Ready to navigate obstacles."

## Overall Discovery Assessment

Use this checklist to assess overall readiness:

### Is the discovery complete for viability call?

**Core Assessment Questions:**
- [ ] Problem is clearly defined and specific (not generic)
- [ ] Problem is backed by evidence (research, data, feedback)
- [ ] Expected outcomes are measurable with realistic targets
- [ ] Team and stakeholders are aligned on problem and approach
- [ ] Use cases are prioritized and well-defined
- [ ] Current and future states are clear
- [ ] Success metrics are defined with baseline and target
- [ ] Key risks are identified with mitigation plans
- [ ] Team is ready to move to solution design
- [ ] Would an informed executive approve this based on evidence?

**If all boxes checked: ✓ Ready for viability call**

**If 2-3 boxes unchecked: ⚠️ Needs work in these areas**

**If more boxes unchecked: ❌ Return to discovery**

## Providing Feedback

### Feedback Template

When assessing a discovery, use this structure:

```
## Overall Assessment: [Level - Insufficient/Needs Work/Good/Excellent]

### What's Strong ✓
- [Section]: [What's working well]
- [Section]: [What's working well]

### What Needs Work ⚠️
- [Section]: [Specific gap or weakness]
- [Section]: [Specific gap or weakness]

### What's Missing ❌
- [Section]: [Required element not present]

### Next Steps
1. [Priority 1 - what to do first]
2. [Priority 2 - what to do next]
3. [Priority 3 - nice to have]

### Ready For?
- [ ] Viability call (if all critical elements strong)
- [ ] More discovery work (specify what)
- [ ] Design (if Level 3+ on all critical sections)
```

### Example Feedback

```
## Overall Assessment: Needs Work ⚠️

### What's Strong ✓
- **Business Problem**: Clear statement with specific user impact and concrete examples
- **Stakeholders**: Good representation from users, business, and technical teams

### What Needs Work ⚠️
- **Expected Outcomes**: Metrics defined but need baseline values. "Reduce time to <1 hour" is good, but baseline should be explicit.
- **Use Cases**: Prioritization is unclear. Which are must-have for launch?

### What's Missing ❌
- **Measurement Plan**: No details on how you'll track metrics post-launch
- **Risks**: No documented risks or open questions

### Next Steps
1. Add baseline numbers to outcomes ("from 8 hours to <1 hour")
2. Prioritize use cases (must-have, should-have, nice-to-have)
3. Define measurement approach (who tracks, how often, what tools)
4. Document risks and mitigation plans

### Ready For?
- [ ] Viability call (return after strengthening gaps)
- [x] More discovery work (focus on outcomes, risks, measurement)
- [ ] Design (not yet)

**Estimated:** 1-2 weeks of discovery refinement, then ready for viability call
```

## Common Patterns

### Discovery Ready for Viability Call

- Problem statement is specific and evidence-backed
- Outcomes are measurable with realistic targets
- Use cases are clear and prioritized
- Team is aligned (or misalignment is explicit and planned to address)
- Risks are identified with mitigation
- Measurement approach is defined

### Discovery Not Yet Ready

- Problem is vague or unvalidated
- Outcomes are aspirational without concrete targets
- Use cases are unclear or not prioritized
- Team is not aligned (and no plan to align)
- No risk discussion
- Measurement is "we'll figure it out later"

### High-Quality Discovery Signals

- Evidence is cited consistently throughout
- Team is actively engaged in discovery
- Assumptions are explicitly called out
- Stakeholders have been involved early
- Data is used to inform decisions
- Edge cases and risks are considered
- Future state improvement is clear
- Outcomes are tied to business value
