# North Star Vision Workshop - Requirements & Design Rationale

**Project:** North Star Vision Workshop Facilitation Plan
**Created:** [Date]
**Purpose:** Document the requirements gathering process and how user inputs shaped the workshop design

---

## Initial User Request

**User's Original Prompt:**
> "Act as a facilitator for product design and development. Create a facilitation template using Luma techniques and others recommended to plan a workshop for a North Star vision session. The outcomes of the session would help create the initial foundations of a product vision and mission, a 5-year roadmap, capabilities roadmap, gap analysis to use to kickstart product team discoveries to launch us into our ideal state. Ask me questions to help develop this plan and the templates."

---

## Requirements Gathering - User Responses

### Question Set 1: Context & Scope

**Q: What is the current state of your product vision and strategy?**
- **Answer:** Refining existing strategy
- **Implication:** Workshop should build on existing work, not start from zero. Pre-work should include review of current materials. Activities should focus on refinement and extension rather than creation from scratch.

**Q: How much time do you have allocated for this workshop?**
- **Answer:** Half-day (3-4 hours)
- **Implication:** Need to be highly focused and efficient. Can't deep-dive on every topic. Must prioritize outputs and use pre-work to save time. Chose 3.5-hour structure with tight timeboxing.

**Q: Who will be participating in this workshop?**
- **Answer:** 5-10 people
- **Implication:** Small enough for intimate discussion and quick consensus. Can use full-group activities rather than always breaking out. Decided on mix of individual, pair, small group, and whole group activities.

**Q: What mix of roles will be in the room?**
- **Answer:** Combination of Product & Engineering Leadership, Team members, and strategy support (Service Designer, journey architect, researchers)
- **Implication:** Diverse perspectives mean need structured frameworks to channel different viewpoints productively. Must balance strategic thinking (leadership) with tactical reality (team members) and user insight (researchers/designers). Chose activities that honor all perspectives.

---

### Question Set 2: Priorities & Challenges

**Q: Which outputs are absolutely critical to complete during the workshop versus ones you could develop further afterward?**
- **Answer:** North Star vision alignment (Must have), 5-year roadmap themes (Must have), detailed capabilities roadmap (Must have)
- **Implication:** All three outputs are critical, which is ambitious for 3.5 hours. Designed phases to build sequentially (vision → roadmap → capabilities) so each informs the next. Accepted that outputs will be drafts requiring post-workshop refinement.

**Q: What is your biggest challenge or concern for this workshop?**
- **Answer:** Balancing ambition with reality, priority conflicts/alignment, and ensuring next steps for Q2 OKR planning
- **Design Response:**
  - **Balancing ambition with reality:** Built in "constraints" review at start (Frame 2), reality-checking questions during roadmap sequencing, and 2x2 prioritization matrix for Q2
  - **Priority conflicts/alignment:** Used dot voting for democratic decision-making, structured frameworks (Horizons) to organize thinking, and explicit check-in/check-out to surface tensions
  - **Translating to action:** Dedicated final phase (50 min) for Q2 priority setting, "working backwards" cascade from vision to action, and owner assignments with deadlines

---

### Additional Context Provided

**User Clarification:**
> "For clarification or as pre-work, our team will need to assess current gaps and current work in flight. This will be hybrid working session, so we would use Mural and Teams. Outcomes are drafts of the Vision, Roadmap, capabilities roadmap, and assessment of what will be needed to prioritize for Q2 OKR Planning."

**Design Implications:**
1. **Pre-work requirement:** Created comprehensive pre-work template with gap analysis and work-in-flight assessment (30-45 min time commitment)
2. **Hybrid format:** Designed all activities to work in both physical and virtual environments. Used Mural as single source of truth. Included Teams-specific facilitation notes (breakout rooms, chat).
3. **Draft outputs:** Set expectation throughout that workshop produces drafts, with assigned owners for refinement post-workshop
4. **Q2 OKR connection:** Created explicit "Vision-to-Action Cascade" framework linking 5-year vision → Year 1 goals → Q2 priorities

## How Requirements Shaped the Design

### Overall Structure: 4 Phases

The 4-phase structure directly addresses the user's outputs and challenges:

1. **Phase 1: Connect & Ground (30 min)**
   - *Addresses:* Need for alignment
   - *Activities:* Check-in, pre-work synthesis
   - *Rationale:* Build trust and shared context before strategic work

2. **Phase 2: Envision the North Star (60 min)**
   - *Addresses:* Critical output #1 (Vision), ambition vs. reality tension
   - *Activities:* Future-back visioning, vision drafting, consensus-building
   - *Rationale:* Start with inspiration before constraints; use structured framework (WHO/WHAT/WHY/HOW) to channel creativity

3. **Phase 3: Map the Journey (70 min)**
   - *Addresses:* Critical outputs #2 and #3 (Roadmap, Capabilities), priority conflicts
   - *Activities:* Horizons mapping, capability mapping, prioritization
   - *Rationale:* Break 5-year plan into digestible horizons; connect themes to capabilities; use voting to surface and resolve conflicts

4. **Phase 4: Translate to Action (50 min)**
   - *Addresses:* Q2 OKR planning need, translating vision to action
   - *Activities:* Q2 priority setting, 2x2 matrix, owner assignments
   - *Rationale:* Ensure workshop doesn't end in abstraction; create direct line from vision to next quarter's work

### Time Allocation Decisions

Given tight 3.5-hour constraint:
- **Reduced:** Gallery walks (10 min vs typical 15-20), individual reflection times (5 min vs typical 10), break (10 min vs typical 15)
- **Increased:** Synthesis and prioritization activities (to address alignment challenge)
- **Cut:** Deep capability planning (moved to post-workshop refinement), scenario planning (would add 45+ min)
- **Protected:** Check-in/check-out (critical for hybrid), Q2 priority setting (critical output), break (energy management)

### Activity Selection Rationale

| Activity | Why Chosen | Alternative Considered |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Future-back visioning | Encourages ambition (address "ambition vs reality" by starting with ambition) | Start from current state (rejected as too constraining) |
| Three Horizons framework | Organizes 5-year thinking, shows dependencies, balances short and long term | Quarterly roadmap (rejected as too tactical for vision workshop) |
| Dot voting | Democratic, surfaces conflicts quickly, works in Mural | Consensus discussion (rejected as too time-consuming for 10 people) |
| Small group breakouts | Honors diverse perspectives, increases participation | Fishbowl (rejected as not hybrid-friendly) |
| 2x2 prioritization matrix | Visual, fast, directly addresses "priority conflicts" | Weighted scoring (rejected as too complex for time available) |
| Working backwards cascade | Explicit link from vision to Q2 action (addresses "translation" challenge) | Direct OKR drafting (rejected as skips strategic connection) |

## Luma Techniques Applied

Based on user's request for "Luma techniques and others," integrated these Luma principles:

### 1. Connection Before Content
- **Applied:** Check-in activity to share hopes (builds psychological safety)
- **Applied:** Check-out activity to reflect (creates closure)
- **Applied:** Pair shares before full group (safer to test ideas)

### 2. Energy Management
- **Applied:** Varied modalities (solo → pairs → small group → whole group)
- **Applied:** Strategic break at midpoint (after intense visioning work)
- **Applied:** Movement activities (gallery walk)
- **Applied:** Silent reflection periods (cognitive rest)

### 3. Productive Conflict
- **Applied:** Named tensions explicitly in Current State Synthesis
- **Applied:** Parking lot for scope management
- **Applied:** Dot voting to surface and democratize disagreement
- **Applied:** "Disagree and commit" framework in facilitation tips

### 4. Visual Thinking
- **Applied:** All work happens on Mural board (shared visual space)
- **Applied:** Templates, colors, icons for clarity
- **Applied:** Real-time clustering and connection-drawing
- **Applied:** 2x2 matrix, horizons timeline, dependency diagrams

### 5. Bias Toward Action
- **Applied:** Every phase produces concrete output
- **Applied:** Owner assignments with deadlines before closing
- **Applied:** Direct connection from vision to Q2 OKRs
- **Applied:** Next steps documented and scheduled

## Additional Facilitation Frameworks Incorporated

Beyond Luma, integrated these facilitation best practices:

### Liberating Structures
- **1-2-4-All pattern:** Individual → Pairs → Small groups → Whole group (used in vision drafting)
- **Impromptu Networking:** Pair shares for idea generation

### Design Thinking
- **Diverge-Converge:** Brainstorm widely (individual stickies), then cluster and prioritize
- **Bias toward action:** Prototyping (draft vision), testing (reality checks), iterating (refinement owners)

### Agile Retrospective Techniques
- **Dot voting:** Democratic prioritization
- **Timeboxing:** Strict adherence to activity durations
- **Parking lot:** Capture without derailing

### Strategic Planning Frameworks
- **Three Horizons:** McKinsey framework for organizing innovation across time
- **Working Backwards:** Amazon technique for connecting vision to action
- **Capability-based planning:** Enterprise architecture approach

## Design Decisions Summary

| Requirement | Design Decision | Rationale |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Half-day timeframe | 3.5 hours with aggressive timeboxing | Realistic with break; 3 hours too rushed, 4 hours too long |
| 5-10 participants | Mix of full-group and small breakouts | Small enough for intimacy, large enough for diversity |
| Hybrid format | Mural as single source of truth | Equalizes experience for remote and in-person |
| Refining existing strategy | Pre-work includes review of current materials | Builds on what exists rather than starting over |
| Must-have outputs | Sequential phases building on each other | Vision informs roadmap, roadmap informs capabilities, all inform Q2 |
| Ambition vs reality tension | Start with aspiration (future-back), reality-check later | Avoid constraining creativity too early |
| Priority conflicts | Dot voting + structured frameworks | Democratic and data-driven conflict resolution |
| Q2 OKR connection | Dedicated 50-min final phase | Non-negotiable to ensure workshop drives action |
| Diverse roles | Activities honor different perspectives | Service designers contribute to vision, engineers to capabilities, etc. |

## Success Criteria Alignment

The workshop design aims to achieve:

### User's Stated Outcomes
- ✅ **Initial foundations of product vision and mission** → Phase 2 produces draft North Star vision with WHO/WHAT/WHY/HOW elements
- ✅ **5-year roadmap** → Phase 3 produces roadmap themes across 3 horizons (Years 1-2, 3-4, 5)
- ✅ **Capabilities roadmap** → Phase 3 maps capabilities by theme with gaps and timelines
- ✅ **Gap analysis** → Pre-work + synthesis identifies and prioritizes gaps
- ✅ **Kickstart product team discoveries** → Q2 priorities assigned to owners who will develop detailed OKRs
- ✅ **Launch into ideal state** → North Star vision defines ideal state; roadmap shows path to get there

### User's Stated Challenges
- ✅ **Balance ambition with reality** → Future-back visioning (ambition) + reality-check activities (constraints, feasibility)
- ✅ **Navigate priority conflicts** → Dot voting, 2x2 matrix, structured decision frameworks
- ✅ **Ensure next steps** → Owner assignments, deadlines, explicit Q2 OKR planning kickoff scheduled

### Additional Implied Needs
- ✅ **Alignment across roles** → Check-in/check-out, inclusive participation, consensus-building activities
- ✅ **Hybrid effectiveness** → All activities designed for Mural + Teams, tested for virtual accessibility
- ✅ **Time efficiency** → Pre-work saves 45+ min, tight timeboxing, parallel breakouts where possible
- ✅ **Actionable outputs** → Every phase produces documented artifact; post-workshop template structures deliverables

## Customization Options

If user's situation changes, here's how to adapt:

### If More Time Available (Full Day)
- Add: Deeper gap analysis workshop component (30 min)
- Add: Customer/user research review (45 min)
- Add: Detailed Year 1 quarterly roadmap (60 min)
- Add: Scenario planning exercises (45 min)
- Extend: Vision drafting to include messaging (30 min)

### If Less Time Available (2 Hours)
- Focus: Vision + Year 1 themes only
- Cut: Capabilities roadmap (do as separate session)
- Cut: Gallery walk
- Reduce: Individual reflection times to 3 min
- Combine: Phases 3 and 4 into single phase

### If Larger Group (15+ People)
- Add: Co-facilitator
- Increase: Breakout groups (3-4 groups instead of 2)
- Extend: Report-out times
- Modify: Pre-work to include pre-alignment on vision elements

### If Different Format (Fully Remote or Fully In-Person)
- Remote: Add virtual energizers, more frequent breaks, assign tech support role
- In-Person: Use physical sticky notes, wall space for boards, larger paper for templates

## Key Assumptions Made

1. **Team has basic strategic planning literacy** (understands OKRs, roadmaps, vision statements)
2. **Pre-work will be completed** (plan has backup if not, but assumes 80%+ completion)
3. **Participants have decision-making authority** (can commit to vision and priorities)
4. **Leadership is present** (for final decisions on priorities and resource allocation)
5. **Follow-through support exists** (owners assigned have capacity to refine drafts)
6. **Tech works** (Mural + Teams accessible and functional; backup plans included)
7. **Psychological safety exists** (team can have honest conversations about gaps and tensions)
