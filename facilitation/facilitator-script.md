# North Star Vision Workshop - Facilitator Script & Run of Show

**Workshop Duration:** 3.5 hours
**Format:** Hybrid (Mural + Teams)
**Participants:** 5-10 people

---

## Pre-Session Setup (15 minutes before start)

### Technical Checklist
- [ ] Open Teams meeting and Mural board
- [ ] Test screen share, audio, video
- [ ] Set up breakout rooms (2 rooms for group work)
- [ ] Test Mural permissions (everyone can edit)
- [ ] Have timer ready and visible
- [ ] Enable recording (if approved by participants)
- [ ] Open parking lot frame in separate browser tab

### Materials Ready
- [ ] Pre-work synthesis slides
- [ ] Printed agenda for in-person folks
- [ ] Backup collaboration tool (Google Jamboard)
- [ ] Contact info for tech support

### Mindset Prep
- [ ] Review participants' pre-work submissions
- [ ] Note any sensitive topics or tensions
- [ ] Prepare to hold space for difficult conversations
- [ ] Remember: you're here to facilitate, not dominate

## PHASE 2: Envision the North Star (60 min)

### 09:30-10:00 | Future-Back Visioning (30 min)

**[09:30] Set Up Activity**

**[Screen share: Mural Frame 3A - Future-Back Visioning]**

💬 **Script:**
> "We're going to do an exercise called 'Future-Back Visioning.' Instead of starting from today and projecting forward, we're going to imagine we've already succeeded - wildly succeeded - and look back from there.
>
> Here's the prompt: **It's 2030, five years from now. We've succeeded beyond our wildest dreams with this product. What does that look like? What impact have we made? What are customers saying? What are we celebrated for?**
>
> I want you to take 5 minutes of **silent reflection** to write your vision story on your individual sticky note on the Mural board. Don't edit yourself - let your imagination run. Ready?
>
> [Start 5-minute timer]"

**[09:30-09:35] Individual Reflection (5 min)**

**[Facilitator actions during silence]:**
- Monitor Mural to see participants writing
- Check chat for tech issues
- Avoid talking (let silence do its work)
- At 4 minutes, give "1 minute left" warning

**[09:35] Transition to Pairs**

💬 **Script:**
> "Time! Great work. Now I'm going to put you into breakout pairs for 10 minutes. In your pair:
> 1. Take turns sharing your 2030 vision story (3-4 minutes each)
> 2. Together, identify 2-3 themes that showed up in both your stories
> 3. Be ready to share those themes with the group when we come back
>
> [Assign pairs - mix roles if possible]
>
> I'll bring you back in 10 minutes. Any questions? Go ahead to your breakout rooms!"

**[09:35-09:45] Pair Share (10 min)**

**[Facilitator actions during breakouts]:**
- Visit each breakout room briefly to check in
- Monitor time
- At 8 minutes, post in all rooms: "2 minutes left"
- At 9 minutes, give 1-minute warning

**[09:45] Bring Everyone Back**

💬 **Script:**
> "Welcome back! I could feel the energy in those conversations. Now let's harvest what emerged.
>
> I'd like each pair to share your top 2-3 themes. As you share, I'll add them to the Mural board and start clustering similar ideas.
>

**[09:45-10:00] Group Share & Clustering (15 min)**

**[Facilitator actions]:**

## Facilitation Tips & Tricks

### Managing Energy
- **Read the room:** If energy drops, call an unplanned 2-min stretch break
- **Vary modalities:** Alternate between solo, pairs, small group, full group
- **Use music:** Play upbeat music during breaks
- **Celebrate progress:** "Look how far we've come!" at midpoint

### Managing Participation
- **Cold call kindly:** "I'd love to hear from someone we haven't heard from yet. [Name], what do you think?"
- **Use chat:** "Drop your answer in chat if you'd prefer not to speak"
- **Break out:** Quieter voices often speak up more in small groups
- **Name the pattern:** "I'm noticing we're hearing mostly from [roles]. Let's hear from [other roles]."

### Managing Conflict
- **Name it:** "I'm sensing tension here. Let's talk about it."
- **Reframe:** "It sounds like you both want [same thing] but have different paths to get there."
- **Parking lot:** "This is important but beyond our scope. Let's parking lot and schedule a separate conversation."
- **Disagree and commit:** "We won't reach full agreement, but can we commit to trying [option] and learning?"

### Managing Time
- **Time signals:** "2 minutes left", "Last thought", "One more comment then we move on"
- **Cut wisely:** "This is rich discussion, but we need to move forward. Let's capture this for later."
- **Bank time:** If an activity finishes early, don't fill the time - bank it for later
- **Extend intentionally:** "This feels critical. I propose we take 5 more minutes here and shorten [other activity]. Agree?"

### Tech Troubleshooting
- **Have backup:** Google Doc for text capture if Mural fails
- **Screen share:** Always share Mural + your screen with timer
- **Breakout rooms:** Test before starting, have co-facilitator help if issues
- **Can't hear/see:** "If you can't hear me, try leaving and rejoining" (post in chat)

## Emergency Scenarios

### Scenario: Running severely over time

**Response:**
1. Call a pause: "We're running over. Let me recalibrate."
2. Triage: "Here's what we've accomplished [X]. Here's what's left [Y, Z]."
3. Offer choice: "We can either end now and schedule follow-up, or extend 30 min. What's the group preference?"
4. If extending, be ruthless: Cut gallery walks, reduce discussion time, combine activities

### Scenario: Major disagreement blocking progress

**Response:**
1. Name it: "We're stuck. Let's pause and acknowledge that."
2. Clarify: "What's the core disagreement? Is it [X] or [Y]?"
3. Seek common ground: "What DO we agree on?"
4. Decision framework: "What information would help us decide?"
5. If still stuck: "Let's identify decision-maker and move on. [Leader], this is your call."

### Scenario: Tech fails completely

**Response:**
1. Stay calm: "Tech happens. Let's pivot."
2. Activate backup: Switch to Google Jamboard or Doc
3. Simplify: Cut visual elements, focus on text capture
4. Adjust expectations: "We'll capture essence now, beautify later."

### Scenario: Key stakeholder disengaged/skeptical

**Response:**
1. Direct invitation: "I notice we haven't heard from you, [Name]. What's your perspective?"
2. Private check-in (chat or breakout): "I'm sensing some hesitation. What would make this more valuable for you?"
3. Adjust approach: If they need more data, add mini-research phase; if they need more structure, tighten framework
4. Accept limitations: Not everyone will be equally engaged - focus on willing participants
