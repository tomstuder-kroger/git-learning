Viability Call
Use the Viability Call to decide:
Do we move forward into Solution Design and engineering, do more discovery, or stop?
This decision should be based on:
•	What we learned in discovery
•	The desired business outcomes
•	The estimated level of effort and timing
Capture the decision and rationale in a short stakeholder/decision maker meeting and document it in the Discovery.
 
## When you might decide not to move forward
You may decide not to proceed to Solution Design (or to pause) when one or more of these are true:
1.	The problem is not worth solving (low impact).
•	Limited business or user value
•	Small, infrequent pain point
•	No meaningful impact on key metrics (revenue, cost, risk, satisfaction, etc.)
2.	The problem is not aligned with strategy.
•	Doesn’t fit current product or company strategy
•	Competes with higher priority initiatives
•	Pulls the team into a market/segment you’re intentionally not targeting
3.	The problem is not well understood yet.
•	Vague or contradictory user needs
•	Insufficient qualitative or quantitative evidence
•	You can’t clearly articulate who has the problem, how often, and what “better” looks like
→ In this case, loop back to deeper problem discovery, not forward to Solution Design.
4.	There is no feasible or ethical way to solve it now.
•	Major technical constraints (technology not ready, prohibitive complexity)
•	Regulatory, legal, or compliance barriers
•	Ethical concerns (privacy, fairness, manipulation) that can’t be addressed acceptably
5.	The timing is wrong.
•	Organization lacks capacity (team bandwidth, budget, expertise)
•	Critical dependencies aren’t in place (platform, data, integrations)
•	Another initiative will soon change the context, making work now likely to be rework
6.	Existing solutions are already “good enough.”
•	The problem is real, but current workarounds or competitor solutions satisfy most users
•	Incremental improvement wouldn’t justify the cost and effort
7.	Stakeholder support is missing.
•	No executive sponsor or product owner willing to fund/champion it
•	Key partners (e.g., legal, sales, ops) oppose moving forward
 
## How to document a “not now” decision
If you decide not to proceed:
•	Document the problem and the reason(s) for not progressing
•	Classify it (e.g., Later, Monitor, Drop)
•	Note any triggers for reconsidering (e.g., new data, strategy shift, platform readiness)
•	Revisit periodically if context, strategy, or feasibility changes
